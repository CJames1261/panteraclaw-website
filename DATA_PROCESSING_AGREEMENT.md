# PanteraClaw — Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

> **Status:** Draft — review with a licensed attorney before use.
> **Version:** 1.0
> **Last Updated:** April 9, 2026

---

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of the Master Services Agreement or Statement of Work between **PanteraClaw** ("Processor") and the Client ("Controller") and governs PanteraClaw's processing of personal data on Client's behalf.

This DPA applies when PanteraClaw processes personal data — including names, email addresses, behavioral data, or any other information relating to identified or identifiable individuals — in the course of providing services to Client.

---

## 1. Definitions

- **"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person as defined under applicable privacy law (including GDPR Art. 4, CCPA, and equivalent state laws).
- **"Processing"** means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, storage, use, transmission, analysis, deletion, or any other handling.
- **"Controller"** means Client — the party that determines the purposes and means of processing Personal Data.
- **"Processor"** means PanteraClaw — the party that processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.
- **"Sub-Processor"** means any third party engaged by PanteraClaw to process Personal Data in connection with the services.
- **"Data Subject"** means the individual whose Personal Data is being processed.
- **"Applicable Privacy Law"** means all applicable data protection and privacy laws, including the GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, and any other applicable state or national privacy law.
- **"Security Incident"** means any unauthorized or unlawful access, disclosure, alteration, loss, or destruction of Personal Data.

---

## 2. Scope and Roles

### 2.1 Processor Role

PanteraClaw processes Personal Data only in its capacity as a **Data Processor** acting on Controller's documented instructions. PanteraClaw does not act as a Data Controller with respect to Client's Personal Data.

### 2.2 Description of Processing

The nature, purpose, categories of Personal Data, and categories of Data Subjects involved in each engagement are described in **Schedule A** (attached to or referenced in the applicable SOW).

### 2.3 Instructions

PanteraClaw will process Personal Data only:
- As instructed in writing by Client (including via the applicable SOW and this DPA).
- As required by applicable law, in which case PanteraClaw will notify Client before processing unless prohibited by law.

If PanteraClaw believes an instruction would violate Applicable Privacy Law, it will promptly notify Client in writing.

---

## 3. PanteraClaw's Obligations

PanteraClaw agrees to:

### 3.1 Confidentiality

Ensure that personnel authorized to process Personal Data are bound by appropriate confidentiality obligations, whether contractual or statutory.

### 3.2 Security

Implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational security measures as described in Section 6 and in PanteraClaw's Security Statement, designed to protect Personal Data against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, and destruction.

### 3.3 Sub-Processors

- Not engage Sub-Processors without Client's prior written authorization (general authorization may be granted in Schedule B).
- Enter into written agreements with Sub-Processors imposing data protection obligations at least as protective as those in this DPA.
- Remain liable to Client for Sub-Processors' compliance with this DPA.
- Maintain and make available an up-to-date list of Sub-Processors upon request.

### 3.4 Data Subject Rights Assistance

Provide reasonable assistance to Client in responding to Data Subject requests (access, correction, deletion, portability, restriction, objection) within the timeframes required by Applicable Privacy Law. PanteraClaw may charge a reasonable fee for assistance beyond routine operations.

### 3.5 Privacy Impact Assessments

Provide reasonable assistance with any Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) or consultations with supervisory authorities required under GDPR Art. 35–36 or equivalent law.

### 3.6 Compliance Records

Maintain records of processing activities as required by GDPR Art. 30 or equivalent obligations and make them available to Client or regulatory authorities upon request.

### 3.7 No Sale or Independent Use

PanteraClaw will not:
- Sell, rent, or share Client's Personal Data with any third party for the third party's own purposes.
- Use Client's Personal Data for any purpose other than performing the contracted services.
- Use Personal Data from one client engagement to benefit another client.
- Train AI or ML models on Client's Personal Data without explicit written consent.

---

## 4. Client's Obligations (Controller)

Client represents and warrants that:

- It has a valid legal basis under Applicable Privacy Law to share Personal Data with PanteraClaw and to instruct PanteraClaw to process it as described.
- It has provided all required notices and obtained all necessary consents from Data Subjects.
- The Personal Data it provides is accurate and collected lawfully.
- It will promptly notify PanteraClaw of any changes to its processing instructions.
- It will not instruct PanteraClaw to process Personal Data in a manner that would violate Applicable Privacy Law.

---

## 5. Security Incident Notification

### 5.1 Detection and Notification

PanteraClaw will notify Client without undue delay — and in any event within **72 hours** of becoming aware — of any confirmed or reasonably suspected Security Incident affecting Client's Personal Data. Notification will be sent to the contact designated in the applicable SOW.

### 5.2 Notification Content

Initial notification will include, to the extent known at the time:
- Nature of the Security Incident
- Categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and Personal Data records affected
- Likely consequences of the incident
- Measures taken or proposed to address the incident

### 5.3 Cooperation

PanteraClaw will cooperate with Client's reasonable requests in investigating the Security Incident and assist with required regulatory notifications.

### 5.4 Client Notification Responsibility

Client is solely responsible for notifying affected Data Subjects and regulatory authorities as required under Applicable Privacy Law. PanteraClaw's notification to Client does not satisfy Client's own regulatory notification obligations.

---

## 6. Security Measures

PanteraClaw implements the following technical and organizational measures (TOMs) for Personal Data processed under this DPA:

| Measure | Description |
|---|---|
| Encryption in transit | TLS 1.2 or higher for all data transmission |
| Encryption at rest | AES-256 or equivalent for stored Personal Data where applicable |
| Access controls | Role-based access; principle of least privilege |
| Authentication | Multi-factor authentication for systems processing Personal Data |
| Audit logging | Access and activity logs retained for security review |
| Vendor management | Sub-Processors screened for security practices |
| Employee training | Personnel with data access trained on data handling obligations |
| Incident response | Documented incident response procedure |
| Data minimization | Only Personal Data necessary for the service is processed |

PanteraClaw reviews and updates these measures periodically. Clients may request a summary of current security practices.

---

## 7. International Data Transfers

Where PanteraClaw transfers Personal Data outside the EEA, UK, or other jurisdiction with transfer restrictions, it will ensure an appropriate transfer mechanism is in place, such as:

- European Commission Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
- UK International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA)
- An applicable adequacy decision
- Other legally recognized transfer mechanism

Details of applicable transfer mechanisms for Sub-Processors are available upon request.

---

## 8. Audits and Inspections

Client may, with **30 days' written notice** and no more than once per calendar year (unless a Security Incident has occurred), audit PanteraClaw's compliance with this DPA, either directly or through an independent third-party auditor bound by confidentiality. PanteraClaw will cooperate reasonably with such audits. Audit costs are borne by Client unless the audit reveals a material breach by PanteraClaw.

---

## 9. Return and Deletion of Personal Data

Upon termination or expiration of the applicable engagement, PanteraClaw will, at Client's election:

- **Return** all Personal Data to Client in a commonly used, machine-readable format within **30 days**.
- **Delete** all Personal Data from PanteraClaw's systems within **30 days**, and provide written certification of deletion upon request.

PanteraClaw may retain Personal Data only where required by applicable law, for the minimum period required, and will continue to protect such data under the terms of this DPA.

---

## 10. Sensitive Data

PanteraClaw does not process the following categories of data under this DPA unless explicitly agreed in writing in the applicable SOW, with enhanced safeguards defined:

- Health or medical information (PHI under HIPAA)
- Biometric data
- Financial account numbers or payment card data (PCI-DSS scope)
- Social Security Numbers or government-issued ID numbers
- Data concerning minors under 13
- Criminal records or background check data
- Special category data under GDPR Art. 9 (racial origin, religion, political opinions, sexual orientation, etc.)

Submission of sensitive data without a written agreement governing its handling is prohibited.

---

## 11. Liability

Each party's liability under this DPA is subject to the limitation of liability provisions in the Master Services Agreement. Where both parties are liable to a Data Subject or regulatory authority for the same damages, liability will be apportioned based on each party's respective fault.

---

## 12. Term and Termination

This DPA remains in effect for the duration of each engagement under which Personal Data is processed and survives termination of the MSA for so long as PanteraClaw retains any Personal Data.
