# PanteraClaw — Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

> **Status:** Draft — review with a licensed attorney before use.
> **Version:** 1.1
> **Last Updated:** April 10, 2026

---

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of the Master Services Agreement or Statement of Work between **PanteraClaw** (doing business as Agentic AI LLC, a Utah limited liability company) ("Processor") and the Client ("Controller") and governs PanteraClaw's processing of personal data on Client's behalf.

This DPA applies when PanteraClaw processes personal data — including names, email addresses, behavioral data, or any other information relating to identified or identifiable individuals — in the course of providing services to Client, such as business web development, database creation, management, and optimization, dashboard and visualization development, agentic workflow design, and advanced analytics.

---

## 1. Definitions

- **"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person as defined under applicable privacy law (including GDPR Art. 4, CCPA/CPRA, UCPA, and equivalent state or federal laws).
- **"Processing"** means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, storage, use, transmission, analysis, deletion, or any other handling.
- **"Controller"** means Client — the party that determines the purposes and means of processing Personal Data.
- **"Processor"** means PanteraClaw — the party that processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.
- **"Sub-Processor"** means any third party engaged by PanteraClaw to process Personal Data in connection with the services.
- **"Data Subject"** means the individual whose Personal Data is being processed.
- **"Applicable Privacy Law"** means all applicable data protection and privacy laws, including the GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, UCPA (Utah Consumer Privacy Act), and any other applicable state, federal, or national privacy law.
- **"Security Incident"** means any unauthorized or unlawful access, disclosure, alteration, loss, or destruction of Personal Data, or any breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data transmitted, stored or otherwise processed.

---

## 2. Scope and Roles

### 2.1 Processor Role

PanteraClaw processes Personal Data only in its capacity as a **Data Processor** acting on Controller's documented instructions. PanteraClaw does not act as a Data Controller with respect to Client's Personal Data, except to the limited extent necessary to comply with applicable law.

### 2.2 Description of Processing

The nature, purpose, categories of Personal Data, and categories of Data Subjects involved in each engagement are described in **Schedule A** (attached to or referenced in the applicable SOW).

### 2.3 Instructions

PanteraClaw will process Personal Data only:
- As instructed in writing by Client (including via the applicable SOW and this DPA).
- As required by applicable law, in which case PanteraClaw will notify Client before processing unless prohibited by law from doing so.

If PanteraClaw believes an instruction would violate Applicable Privacy Law, it will promptly notify Client in writing and may suspend processing until resolved.

---

## 3. PanteraClaw's Obligations

PanteraClaw agrees to:

### 3.1 Confidentiality

Ensure that personnel authorized to process Personal Data have committed in writing to appropriate confidentiality obligations, whether contractual or statutory, and that such obligations survive termination of employment or engagement.

### 3.2 Security

Implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational security measures as described in Section 6 and in PanteraClaw's Security Statement, designed to protect Personal Data against unauthorized or unlawful processing and against accidental loss, destruction, damage, alteration, or disclosure, commensurate with the risks posed by the processing and the nature of the Personal Data.

### 3.3 Sub-Processors

- Not engage any new Sub-Processors without Client's prior written authorization (general authorization may be granted in Schedule B).
- Provide Client with an up-to-date list of current Sub-Processors and notify Client in writing at least 14 days in advance of any intended changes (additions or replacements), allowing Client 14 days to object on reasonable grounds related to data protection.
- Enter into written agreements with Sub-Processors imposing data protection obligations at least as protective as those in this DPA.
- Remain fully liable to Client for any Sub-Processor's compliance with the obligations of this DPA.
- Maintain and make available to Client upon request an up-to-date list of Sub-Processors.

### 3.4 Data Subject Rights Assistance

Provide reasonable assistance to Client in responding to Data Subject requests for access, correction, deletion, portability, restriction, or objection, within the timeframes required by Applicable Privacy Law. PanteraClaw may charge a reasonable fee for assistance beyond routine operations, subject to prior agreement.

### 3.5 Privacy Impact Assessments

Provide reasonable assistance with any Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs), Transfer Impact Assessments (TIAs), or consultations with supervisory authorities required under GDPR Art. 35–36 or equivalent law.

### 3.6 Compliance Records

Maintain records of processing activities as required by GDPR Art. 30 or equivalent obligations and make them available to Client or regulatory authorities upon request.

### 3.7 No Sale or Independent Use

PanteraClaw will not:
- Sell, rent, or share Client's Personal Data with any third party for the third party's own purposes (as "sell" or "share" is defined under CCPA/CPRA or equivalent law).
- Use Client's Personal Data for any purpose other than performing the contracted services.
- Use Personal Data from one client engagement to benefit another client without explicit written consent.
- Train AI or ML models on Client's Personal Data without explicit prior written consent from Client.

## 4. Client's Obligations (Controller)

Client represents and warrants that:
- It has a valid legal basis under Applicable Privacy Law to share Personal Data with PanteraClaw and to instruct PanteraClaw to process it as described.
- It has provided all required notices and obtained all necessary consents, permissions, or authorizations from Data Subjects.
- The Personal Data it provides is accurate, complete, and collected lawfully.
- It will promptly notify PanteraClaw of any changes to its processing instructions or any Security Incidents affecting data shared with PanteraClaw.
- It will not instruct PanteraClaw to process Personal Data in a manner that would violate Applicable Privacy Law.
- It will indemnify and hold harmless PanteraClaw from any claims arising from Client's breach of these obligations.

---

## 5. Security Incident Notification

### 5.1 Detection and Notification

PanteraClaw will notify Client without undue delay — and in any event within **72 hours** of becoming aware — of any confirmed or reasonably suspected Security Incident affecting Client's Personal Data. Notification will be sent to the contact designated in the applicable SOW or in writing by Client.

### 5.2 Notification Content

Initial notification will include, to the extent known at the time:
- Nature of the Security Incident
- Categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and Personal Data records affected
- Likely consequences of the incident
- Measures taken or proposed to address the incident
- Name and contact details of PanteraClaw's data protection officer or equivalent.

PanteraClaw will provide reasonable follow-up information as it becomes available.

### 5.3 Cooperation

PanteraClaw will cooperate with Client's reasonable requests in investigating the Security Incident, remediating its effects, and assisting with required regulatory notifications or communications to Data Subjects.

### 5.4 Client Notification Responsibility

Client is solely responsible for notifying affected Data Subjects and regulatory authorities as required under Applicable Privacy Law. PanteraClaw's notification to Client does not satisfy Client's own regulatory notification obligations.

## 6. Security Measures

PanteraClaw implements the following technical and organizational measures (TOMs) for Personal Data processed under this DPA (these measures are reviewed and updated at least annually):

| Measure | Description |
|---|---|
| Encryption in transit | TLS 1.3 or higher for all data transmission |
| Encryption at rest | AES-256 or equivalent for stored Personal Data |
| Access controls | Role-based access; principle of least privilege; regular access reviews |
| Authentication | Multi-factor authentication (MFA) for all systems processing Personal Data |
| Audit logging | Comprehensive access and activity logs retained for at least 12 months for security review |
| Vendor management | Sub-Processors subject to due diligence and contractual security requirements |
| Employee training | Annual training for personnel with data access on data handling, security, and privacy obligations |
| Incident response | Documented incident response procedure tested annually |
| Data minimization and retention | Only Personal Data necessary for the service is processed; retained only as long as necessary |
| Vulnerability management | Regular scanning, patching, and penetration testing |
| Business continuity | Backups and disaster recovery procedures |

Clients may request a summary of current security practices or evidence of compliance.

## 7. International Data Transfers

PanteraClaw primarily processes Personal Data within the United States. Where PanteraClaw or a Sub-Processor transfers Personal Data subject to GDPR, UK GDPR, or other jurisdictions with transfer restrictions (e.g., from EEA/UK to US), it will ensure an appropriate transfer mechanism is in place, such as:
- European Commission Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or New SCCs (2021)
- UK International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA) or Addendum
- An applicable adequacy decision
- Valid Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)
- Other legally recognized transfer mechanism

Details of applicable transfer mechanisms, including for Sub-Processors, are available upon request. PanteraClaw will notify Client of any changes affecting transfer mechanisms.

---

## 8. Audits and Inspections

Upon Client's reasonable written request and **30 days' prior notice** (and no more than once per 12-month period unless required due to a Security Incident or regulatory mandate), Client may audit PanteraClaw's compliance with this DPA, either directly or through an independent third-party auditor bound by reasonable confidentiality obligations. Audits will be conducted during business hours and limited to data relevant to Client. PanteraClaw will cooperate reasonably and provide information necessary for the audit. Client bears all costs of the audit unless it reveals a material non-compliance by PanteraClaw, in which case PanteraClaw will reimburse reasonable costs.

---

## 9. Return and Deletion of Personal Data

Upon termination or expiration of the applicable engagement, or earlier at Client's written request, PanteraClaw will, at Client's option (specified in writing):
- **Return** all Personal Data to Client in a commonly used, machine-readable format (e.g., CSV, JSON) within **30 days**, and thereafter delete all copies; or
- **Delete** all Personal Data from PanteraClaw's systems within **30 days**.

PanteraClaw will provide written certification of compliance (return and/or deletion) upon Client's request. PanteraClaw may retain Personal Data only where required by applicable law (e.g., tax, accounting, or litigation holds), for the minimum period required, under confidentiality and security obligations no less protective than those in this DPA, after which it will be deleted.

---

## 10. Sensitive Data

PanteraClaw does not process the following categories of sensitive or special category data under this DPA unless explicitly agreed in a written amendment to the applicable SOW, with enhanced safeguards (e.g., additional encryption, access restrictions) defined therein:
- Health or medical information (e.g., PHI under HIPAA)
- Biometric data
- Financial account numbers, payment card data (PCI-DSS scope), or precise geolocation
- Social Security Numbers, driver's license numbers, or government-issued ID numbers
- Data concerning minors under 13 (COPPA)
- Criminal records or background check data
- Special category data under GDPR Art. 9 (e.g., racial/ethnic origin, political opinions, religious/philosophical beliefs, trade union membership, genetic data, biometric data for ID purposes, health data, sex life/orientation)

Client will not submit such data without prior written agreement. Any unauthorized submission does not alter PanteraClaw's non-liability for such data.

## 11. Liability and Indemnification

Each party's liability under this DPA is subject to the limitation of liability provisions in the Master Services Agreement or applicable SOW. Notwithstanding the foregoing, neither party excludes liability for death, personal injury, fraud, or willful misconduct.

PanteraClaw will indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Client from third-party claims arising from PanteraClaw's violation of Applicable Privacy Law or this DPA, subject to the caps in the MSA/SOW.

Client will indemnify, defend, and hold harmless PanteraClaw from third-party claims arising from Client's violation of Applicable Privacy Law, inaccurate or unlawful Personal Data provided by Client, or Client's processing instructions, subject to the caps in the MSA/SOW.

Where both parties are liable to a Data Subject or regulatory authority for the same damages, liability will be apportioned based on each party's respective fault or contribution.

## 12. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This DPA is governed by the laws of the State of Utah, without regard to conflict of laws principles. Any disputes arising under this DPA will be resolved exclusively in the state or federal courts located in Salt Lake County, Utah. For GDPR purposes, the governing law includes the laws of Utah as supplemented by English law to the extent required.
