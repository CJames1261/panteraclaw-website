## 1. Background and Purpose

The Parties have entered into a Services Agreement under which PanteraClaw may create, receive, maintain, or transmit Protected Health Information (PHI) on behalf of the Covered Entity. The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA), as amended by the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act (HITECH), and their implementing regulations (collectively "HIPAA Rules") require that such a relationship be governed by a Business Associate Agreement.

---

## 2. Definitions

Terms used but not otherwise defined in this BAA have the meanings given in the HIPAA Rules (45 C.F.R. Parts 160 and 164).

- **"Protected Health Information" (PHI)** means individually identifiable health information as defined under 45 C.F.R. § 160.103, including electronic PHI (ePHI).
- **"Security Incident"** means the attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of PHI or interference with system operations in an information system containing PHI.
- **"Breach"** has the meaning given in 45 C.F.R. § 164.402.
- **"Subcontractor"** means a person who acts on behalf of the Business Associate and handles PHI.

---

## 3. Permitted Uses and Disclosures of PHI

### 3.1 Permitted Uses

Business Associate may use PHI only:

(a) As necessary to perform the services described in the Services Agreement;
(b) As required by law; or
(c) For Business Associate's proper management and administration, provided disclosures are required by law or Business Associate obtains reasonable assurances from the recipient that the PHI will be held confidentially.

### 3.2 Prohibited Uses

Business Associate will not:

- Use or disclose PHI in any manner not permitted by this BAA or the HIPAA Rules.
- Use PHI for Business Associate's own marketing purposes.
- Sell PHI without written authorization from the Covered Entity.
- Use PHI to train AI or machine learning models without explicit prior written authorization from Covered Entity and appropriate patient authorizations where required.

---

## 4. Business Associate's Obligations

Business Associate agrees to:

### 4.1 Safeguards

Implement and maintain appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHI as required by the HIPAA Security Rule (45 C.F.R. Part 164, Subpart C), including:

- Conducting periodic risk assessments
- Implementing access controls and audit logging
- Encrypting ePHI in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256 or equivalent)
- Training workforce members with access to PHI on HIPAA requirements
- Maintaining a documented incident response and breach notification procedure

### 4.2 Subcontractors

Obtain written assurances from any Subcontractor that creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on Business Associate's behalf that the Subcontractor will comply with the same restrictions and conditions that apply to Business Associate under this BAA.

### 4.3 Individual Rights

To the extent Business Associate holds a Designated Record Set, assist Covered Entity in fulfilling individuals' rights under HIPAA, including:

- Right of access (45 C.F.R. § 164.524)
- Right to amendment (45 C.F.R. § 164.526)
- Right to an accounting of disclosures (45 C.F.R. § 164.528)

Business Associate will respond to Covered Entity's requests regarding individual rights within **15 business days**.

### 4.4 Minimum Necessary

Use, disclose, or request only the minimum amount of PHI necessary to accomplish the intended purpose of each use, disclosure, or request.

### 4.5 Accounting of Disclosures

Maintain a record of all disclosures of PHI (other than for treatment, payment, or healthcare operations) and make this record available to Covered Entity upon request.

### 4.6 HITECH Compliance

Comply with applicable requirements of the HITECH Act, including notification requirements and restrictions on the use of PHI for marketing and fundraising purposes.

## 5. Breach and Security Incident Notification

### 5.1 Breach Notification

Business Associate will notify Covered Entity without unreasonable delay and in no event later than **60 days** after discovery of a Breach of Unsecured PHI. Notification will include, to the extent possible:

- The date of the Breach and date of discovery
- Description of the Breach and the PHI involved
- Categories and approximate number of individuals and PHI records affected
- Steps being taken to investigate and mitigate harm
- Contact information for questions

*Note: The 60-day window is the HIPAA maximum. Business Associate will endeavor to notify as quickly as possible, and in no event later than 72 hours for confirmed Breaches where feasible.*

### 5.2 Security Incidents

Business Associate will report to Covered Entity any Security Incident (including unsuccessful attempts that could indicate a pattern) of which it becomes aware, within a reasonable timeframe.

### 5.3 Covered Entity's Notification Obligations

Covered Entity is responsible for notifying affected individuals and the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) as required by 45 C.F.R. §§ 164.404 and 164.408. Business Associate will cooperate with Covered Entity in fulfilling these obligations.

---

## 6. Covered Entity's Obligations

Covered Entity agrees to:

- Notify Business Associate of any limitations in its Notice of Privacy Practices that affect Business Associate's permitted uses and disclosures of PHI.
- Notify Business Associate of any restriction on use or disclosure of PHI to which Covered Entity has agreed.
- Not request Business Associate to use or disclose PHI in any manner that would violate the HIPAA Rules.
- Obtain all necessary patient authorizations for uses of PHI that require individual authorization.

---

## 7. Term and Termination

### 7.1 Term

This BAA is effective upon execution and remains in force for the duration of the Services Agreement.

### 7.2 Termination for Cause

Either Party may terminate this BAA and the Services Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party has materially breached this BAA and failed to cure the breach within **30 days** of written notice. If cure is not possible, the non-breaching Party may terminate immediately.

### 7.3 Effect of Termination

Upon termination, Business Associate will:

- Return to Covered Entity or destroy all PHI in any form that Business Associate or its Subcontractors maintain, and certify in writing that this has been done.
- If return or destruction is not feasible, extend the protections of this BAA indefinitely to any retained PHI and limit further uses to those that make return or destruction infeasible.

---

## 8. Audit and Inspection

Business Associate will make its internal practices, books, and records relating to PHI available to the U.S. Secretary of Health and Human Services (HHS) for purposes of determining compliance with the HIPAA Rules. Business Associate will notify Covered Entity of any such request or inspection.

PanteraClaw does not handle PHI under any engagement without a fully executed BAA reviewed by legal counsel.**
