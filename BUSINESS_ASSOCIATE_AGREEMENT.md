## 1. Background and Purpose

The Parties have entered into a Services Agreement under which Business Associate may create, receive, maintain, or transmit Protected Health Information (PHI) on behalf of the Covered Entity. The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA), as amended by the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act (HITECH), and their implementing regulations (collectively "HIPAA Rules") require that such a relationship be governed by a Business Associate Agreement.<a href="https://arxiv.org/html/2504.17669v1" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[1]</a>

---

## 2. Definitions

Terms used but not otherwise defined in this BAA have the meanings given in the HIPAA Rules (45 C.F.R. Parts 160 and 164).

- **"Protected Health Information" (PHI)** means individually identifiable health information as defined under 45 C.F.R. § 160.103, including electronic PHI (ePHI).
- **"Security Incident"** means the attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of PHI or interference with system operations in an information system containing PHI, as defined in 45 C.F.R. § 164.304.
- **"Breach"** has the meaning given in 45 C.F.R. § 164.402.
- **"Subcontractor"** means any person or entity who acts on behalf of the Business Associate and creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI.<a href="https://www.kiteworks.com/hipaa-compliance/ai-agents-hipaa-phi-access/" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[2]</a>
- **"Designated Record Set"** has the meaning given in 45 C.F.R. § 164.501.

## 3. Permitted Uses and Disclosures of PHI

### 3.1 Permitted Uses

Business Associate may use or disclose PHI only:

(a) To perform services described in the Services Agreement;  
(b) As required by law; or  
(c) For Business Associate's proper management and administration or to carry out its legal responsibilities, provided any disclosure is either required by law or Business Associate obtains reasonable assurances from the recipient that PHI will remain confidential and be used or further disclosed only as required by law or for the purpose for which it was disclosed.<a href="https://arxiv.org/html/2504.17669v1" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[1]</a>

### 3.2 Prohibited Uses

Business Associate will not:

- Use or disclose PHI in any manner not permitted by this BAA or the HIPAA Rules.  
- Use or disclose PHI for Business Associate's own marketing purposes or to sell PHI.  
- Use PHI to train AI, machine learning models, or agentic workflows without **explicit prior written authorization** from Covered Entity and any required patient authorizations under 45 C.F.R. § 164.508.<a href="https://arxiv.org/html/2504.17669v1" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[1]</a><a href="https://www.techtarget.com/healthtechsecurity/feature/Key-HIPAA-compliance-considerations-for-agentic-AI-tools" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[3]</a><a href="https://quickblox.com/answers/ai-medical-assistant-hipaa-compliant/" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[5]</a>

## 4. Business Associate's Obligations

Business Associate agrees to:

### 4.1 Safeguards

Implement and maintain appropriate administrative, physical, and technical **safeguards to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHI** as required by the HIPAA Security Rule (45 C.F.R. Part 164, Subpart C), **including but not limited to**:

- Conducting **periodic** technical and non-technical risk assessments per 45 C.F.R. § 164.308(a)(1)<a href="https://securiti.ai/ai-hipaa-compliance/" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[4]</a>  
- Implementing **access controls** (e.g., unique user IDs, role-based access) and **audit controls** per 45 C.F.R. §§ 164.312(a)(2)-(3)<a href="https://www.kiteworks.com/hipaa-compliance/ai-agents-hipaa-phi-access/" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[2]</a>  
- **Encrypting ePHI** in transit (e.g., TLS 1.3) and at rest (e.g., AES-256 or FIPS 140-3 validated equivalent) per 45 C.F.R. § 164.312(e)(2)<a href="https://www.kiteworks.com/hipaa-compliance/ai-agents-hipaa-phi-access/" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[2]</a>  
- Training workforce members with access to PHI on HIPAA policies and procedures per 45 C.F.R. § 164.308(a)(5)  
- Maintaining **documented** security incident procedures and **contingency plans** per 45 C.F.R. §§ 164.308(a)(6)-(7)<a href="https://arxiv.org/html/2504.17669v1" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[1]</a>

### 4.2 Subcontractors

Prior to permitting any Subcontractor to create, receive, maintain, or transmit PHI, obtain **written assurances** that the Subcontractor will comply with the same restrictions, conditions, and requirements that apply to Business Associate under this BAA. Provide Covered Entity with copies of such assurances upon request.<a href="https://www.kiteworks.com/hipaa-compliance/ai-agents-hipaa-phi-access/" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[2]</a><a href="https://securiti.ai/ai-hipaa-compliance/" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[4]</a>

### 4.3 Individual Rights

To the extent Business Associate maintains PHI in a **Designated Record Set**, assist Covered Entity in fulfilling individuals' rights under **45 C.F.R. §§ 164.524-528** (access, amendment, accounting of disclosures, restrictions, confidential communications). Business Associate will respond to Covered Entity's written requests **within 30 days** (or 60 days with one extension).<a href="https://arxiv.org/html/2504.17669v1" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[1]</a>

### 4.4 Minimum Necessary

Use, disclose, or request only the **minimum necessary** PHI to accomplish the intended purpose per 45 C.F.R. § 164.502(b) and § 164.514(d).<a href="https://securiti.ai/ai-hipaa-compliance/" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[4]</a>

### 4.5 Accounting of Disclosures

Maintain records of disclosures of PHI (except to treatment, payment, health care operations, or as permitted under 45 C.F.R. § 164.528(a)(1)(iv-vi)) for **6 years** and provide to Covered Entity upon request.<a href="https://arxiv.org/html/2504.17669v1" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[1]</a>

### 4.6 HITECH Compliance

Comply with HITECH Act requirements, including breach notification, marketing restrictions (45 C.F.R. § 164.508(b)(4)), and fundraising limitations (45 C.F.R. § 164.514(f)).<a href="https://arxiv.org/html/2504.17669v1" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[1]</a>

## 5. Breach and Security Incident Notification

### 5.1 Breach Notification

Business Associate will notify Covered Entity **without unreasonable delay and in no event later than 60 calendar days** after discovery of any **Breach of Unsecured PHI** per 45 C.F.R. § 164.410. Notification will include:

- Date of Breach and discovery  
- Description of what happened and PHI involved (types and number of records)  
- Actual or suspected identity of unauthorized person(s)  
- Steps taken to investigate/remediate  
- Contact point for questions  

Business Associate **aims to notify within 72 hours** where feasible.<a href="https://arxiv.org/html/2504.17669v1" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[1]</a><a href="https://www.kiteworks.com/hipaa-compliance/ai-agents-hipaa-phi-access/" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[2]</a>

### 5.2 Security Incidents

Report to Covered Entity **all Security Incidents** (including patterns of unsuccessful attempts) **immediately** or within **24 hours** of awareness.<a href="https://www.kiteworks.com/hipaa-compliance/ai-agents-hipaa-phi-access/" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[2]</a>

### 5.3 Cooperation

Business Associate will cooperate fully with Covered Entity's notifications to individuals/HHS and mitigation efforts. Covered Entity retains responsibility for such notifications per 45 C.F.R. §§ 164.404-412.<a href="https://arxiv.org/html/2504.17669v1" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[1]</a>

## 6. Covered Entity's Obligations

Covered Entity agrees to:

- Notify Business Associate of any **limitations** in its Notice of Privacy Practices per 45 C.F.R. § 164.520.  
- Notify Business Associate of any **restrictions** on PHI use/disclosure per 45 C.F.R. § 164.522.  
- Not request Business Associate to use/disclose PHI in violation of HIPAA Rules.  
- Provide Business Associate necessary patient authorizations.<a href="https://arxiv.org/html/2504.17669v1" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[1]</a>

## 7. Term and Termination

### 7.1 Term

Effective upon execution; continues through the Services Agreement term.

### 7.2 Termination for Cause

Either Party may terminate immediately upon written notice if the other **materially breaches** this BAA and fails to cure within **30 days** (or immediately if incurable).<a href="https://arxiv.org/html/2504.17669v1" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[1]</a>

### 7.3 Effect of Termination

Business Associate will **return or destroy** (with written certification) all PHI within **30 days**. If infeasible, protections continue indefinitely; uses limited to return/destruction purposes.<a href="https://arxiv.org/html/2504.17669v1" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[1]</a>

## 8. Audit and Inspection

Make books/records relating to PHI use/disclosures available to **HHS** (or Covered Entity on HHS's behalf) upon request, to verify HIPAA compliance. Notify Covered Entity of any such request within **3 business days**.<a href="https://arxiv.org/html/2504.17669v1" class="citation-link" target="_blank" style="vertical-align: super; font-size: 0.8em; margin-left: 3px;">[1]</a>
