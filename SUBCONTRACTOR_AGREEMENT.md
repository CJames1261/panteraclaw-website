## 3. Independent Contractor Status

### 3.1 No Employment Relationship

Contractor is an independent contractor, not an employee, agent, partner, or joint venturer of Company. This Agreement does not create an employment relationship. Company will not:

- Withhold income taxes or FICA from Contractor's compensation
- Provide workers' compensation, unemployment insurance, or employee benefits
- Direct or control the means and methods by which Contractor performs the Services, only the results

### 3.2 No Authority to Bind

Contractor has no authority to enter into contracts, make commitments, or otherwise bind Company without prior written authorization.

### 3.3 Other Clients

Contractor may perform services for other clients, provided doing so does not create a conflict of interest with Company or violate any other term of this Agreement.

## 4. Intellectual Property

### 4.1 Work Product Ownership

All work product, deliverables, code, models, documentation, designs, and other materials created by Contractor in the performance of Services under this Agreement ("Work Product") are considered works made for hire to the maximum extent permitted by law and shall be the sole and exclusive property of Company.

To the extent any Work Product does not qualify as a work made for hire, Contractor hereby irrevocably assigns to Company all right, title, and interest in and to such Work Product, including all intellectual property rights therein.

### 4.2 Pre-Existing IP

Contractor retains ownership of any tools, frameworks, libraries, or other intellectual property created independently of this Agreement ("Pre-Existing IP"). If Work Product incorporates Pre-Existing IP, Contractor grants Company a non-exclusive, perpetual, royalty-free license to use such Pre-Existing IP as part of the Work Product.

Contractor must disclose any Pre-Existing IP incorporated into deliverables before delivery.

### 4.3 Moral Rights

To the extent permitted by law, Contractor waives all moral rights in the Work Product in favor of Company.

### 4.4 Third-Party Code

Contractor will not incorporate third-party code, open-source software, or AI-generated content into deliverables without disclosing the source and applicable license terms. Contractor will not incorporate code or content that infringes any third-party intellectual property rights.

### 4.5 AI-Generated Work

If Contractor uses AI tools (e.g., GitHub Copilot, ChatGPT, Claude) to generate any portion of deliverables, Contractor must: (a) disclose this use to Company; (b) review and take responsibility for all AI-generated content; and (c) ensure it does not introduce copyright, license, or quality issues.

## 5. Confidentiality

### 5.1 Obligation

Contractor will hold all Confidential Information of Company (and Company's clients) in strict confidence, use it solely to perform the Services, and not disclose it to any third party without Company's prior written consent.

### 5.2 Definition

"Confidential Information" includes: client identities and data, project details, source code, technical architecture, business strategies, pricing, and any information designated as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential.

### 5.3 Client Data

Contractor may be exposed to client data in the course of performing Services. Contractor agrees to handle all client data in accordance with Company's instructions, applicable privacy law, and any Data Processing Agreement applicable to the engagement.

### 5.4 No Personal Use

Contractor will not use Confidential Information for personal benefit or to compete with Company.

### 5.5 Duration

Confidentiality obligations survive termination of this Agreement for **three (3) years**, or indefinitely for trade secrets.

## 6. Non-Solicitation

During the term of this Agreement and for **twelve (12) months** after termination, Contractor will not:

- Directly solicit, recruit, or hire any employee or contractor of Company.
- Directly solicit any client of Company with whom Contractor had contact during the engagement, for the purpose of providing competing services.

*Note: Non-solicitation of clients is distinct from non-compete. Contractor retains the right to compete generally in the marketplace.*

### 10.1 Term

### 10.3 Termination for Cause

Either Party may terminate immediately if the other materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within **7 days** of written notice.

### 10.4 Survival

Sections 4 (IP), 5 (Confidentiality), 6 (Non-Solicitation), 8 (Indemnification), and 11 (Governing Law) survive termination.

## Notes for Attorney Review

- [ ] Review worker classification carefully — misclassifying employees as contractors carries significant IRS and state tax liability. Confirm this structure is appropriate for the actual working relationship.
- [ ] Non-solicitation enforceability varies by state — Utah is generally more permissive than California (where non-solicitation of clients may be unenforceable). Confirm for your jurisdiction.
- [ ] Review work-for-hire language — not all work qualifies as work-for-hire under copyright law; the assignment clause in 4.1 is the backstop.
- [ ] If Contractor will access client systems or client data, add reference to your DPA and require Contractor to comply with data handling obligations.
- [ ] Consider requiring Contractor to carry professional liability (E&O) insurance for higher-risk engagements.
- [ ] Review AI-generated work disclosure requirement (Section 4.5) — this is a newer area with evolving IP law implications.
